LINUX

Linux, created by Linus Torvalds in 1991, started as a free, open-source operating system kernel. It was built on Unix principles and rapidly gained popularity among developers. Over time, it evolved with contributions from global communities, becoming a powerful alternative to proprietary systems, widely used in servers, desktops, and embedded devices.

The vision of Linux is to create a free, open-source operating system that is community-driven, secure, and adaptable across various devices. Linux has become a key platform for innovation, with contributions from developers around the world.

The goals of Linux include being a fast, reliable, and secure operating system while remaining open and flexible. It aims to provide a platform that is efficient, scalable, and compatible across a variety of hardware architectures.
